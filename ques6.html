List any 5 semantic tags in HTML along with their descriptions

Here are five commonly used semantic HTML tags along with their descriptions:
1. <header >: It represents the introductory or navigational section of a document or a specific section within a document. It typically includes the site's logo, site title, navigation menus, or other introductory content.
2. <nav >: It is used to define a section of a document that contains navigation links. It is typically used to mark a collection of links that allow users to navigate within the website or to other related pages.
3. <main >: It represents the main content area of a document. It should contain the primary content that is unique to the document, excluding headers, footers, or navigation sections. There should be only one

relement per page.
4.<article >: It represents a self-contained composition that can be independently distributed or reusable, such as a blog post, news article, forum post, or comment. It should make sense on its own and can be syndicated or independently consumed.
5. <footer >: It represents the footer section of a document or a specific section within a document. It typically contains information about the author, copyright notices, contact information, or related links. It is commonly placed at the end of the page or at the end of individual sections.
